About the Caldecott Medal

The Randolph Caldecott Medal is one of the most prestigious children's book awards in the United States. Named after 19th-century English illustrator Randolph Caldecott, it has been awarded annually since 1938 by the Association for Library Service to Children (ALSC), a division of the American Library Association (ALA).

The medal is given to the illustrator of the most distinguished American picture book for children published during the preceding year. The committee also awards Caldecott Honor Books to runners-up deemed truly distinguished.

What is the Caldecott Medal?

The Caldecott Medal is awarded annually by the American Library Association to the illustrator of the most distinguished American picture book for children. It has been given every year since 1938.

What is the difference between a Caldecott Medal and a Caldecott Honor?

The Caldecott Medal goes to the single most distinguished picture book each year. Caldecott Honor Books are awarded to between one and five additional books the committee deems truly distinguished.

Are Caldecott books good for all ages?

Caldecott books are picture books primarily intended for children ages 2–8, but many are enjoyed by older children and adults. The award focuses on illustration quality rather than reading level.
